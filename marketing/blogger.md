# Blogger article draft

## Title

Why Seattle Startups Should Treat AI “Skills” Like Progressive Disclosure (Not a Second System Prompt)

## Body

If your team pastes ever-growing instructions into every agent session, you are paying twice: in **tokens** and in **quality**, because the model has less room for the code, logs, and decisions that actually matter.

**Greater Seattle Agent Skills Week** is a one-week program for a small selected group of startups. It is anchored in a clear explainer on how **AI agents and skills** really work in practice—context discipline, **progressive disclosure** (short skill metadata first; full body on demand), and **recursive skill building** (teach the workflow, fix failures with evidence, then encode the successful run).

Over seven days, teams move from auditing noisy global rules to maintaining a **skills catalog** tied to real repositories, verification steps, and metrics—so adoption survives the week after the workshop ends.

Anchor discussion (recommended pre-read): [Greg Isenberg interviews Ross Mike — How AI agents & Claude skills work](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S_oN3vlzpMw).
